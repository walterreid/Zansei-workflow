# Restaurant Marketing Insights

## Industry Pain Points

### Time Constraints
- **Average marketing time**: 10-15 hours per week
- **Peak hours conflict**: Marketing time competes with service hours
- **Staff limitations**: Owners wear many hats, marketing often deprioritized

### Customer Churn
- **High turnover**: Customers try new places frequently
- **Seasonal fluctuations**: Weather, holidays, local events affect traffic
- **Competition**: New restaurants open regularly, established chains compete

### Operational Challenges
- **Menu complexity**: Hard to message when menu changes seasonally
- **Perishable inventory**: Can't easily adjust to demand fluctuations
- **Labor costs**: High staff turnover, training costs

## Budget Realities

### $0-500/month
**Focus**: Free channels
- Google Business Profile optimization
- Organic social media (Instagram, Facebook)
- Email marketing to existing customers
- Local directory listings
- Community partnerships

**Avoid**: Paid ads (insufficient budget for meaningful testing)

### $500-2000/month
**Focus**: Targeted local advertising
- Google Local Services ads
- Facebook/Instagram geo-targeted ads
- Email marketing platform
- Simple loyalty program
- Local event sponsorships

**Test**: 1-2 channels maximum

### $2000+/month
**Focus**: Multi-channel strategy
- Google Ads (search + display)
- Social media advertising
- Email automation
- Loyalty program platform
- PR and influencer partnerships

## Effective Channels

### High ROI Channels
1. **Google Business Profile**: Essential for local discovery
2. **Instagram**: Visual content, food photography, stories
3. **Email marketing**: Repeat customer communication
4. **Local partnerships**: Cross-promote with complementary businesses
5. **Community events**: Farmers markets, festivals, local sponsorships

### Channel-Specific Tactics

#### Google Business Profile
- Post daily specials, events, updates
- Respond to all reviews (positive and negative)
- Add photos regularly (food, ambiance, staff)
- Use posts to highlight menu items, events
- Enable messaging for reservations/questions

#### Instagram
- Post food photos 3-5x per week
- Use Stories for behind-the-scenes, daily specials
- Run polls and Q&A to engage followers
- Use local hashtags (#[city]food, #[neighborhood]eats)
- Partner with local food bloggers/influencers

#### Email Marketing
- Welcome series for new customers
- Weekly newsletter with specials and events
- Birthday rewards and anniversary specials
- Re-engagement campaigns for inactive customers
- Post-dining follow-up asking for reviews

## Success Patterns

### High-Performing Restaurants
- **Consistent posting**: 3-5x per week on primary platform
- **Community involvement**: Sponsor local events, partner with neighbors
- **Customer engagement**: Respond to reviews, engage on social media
- **Clear value proposition**: What makes them unique (cuisine, service, atmosphere)
- **Loyalty programs**: Simple punch cards or digital rewards

### Common Mistakes
- Inconsistent social media presence
- Ignoring negative reviews
- No email capture system
- Generic messaging (not differentiating from competitors)
- Ignoring local SEO opportunities

## Industry Benchmarks

### Customer Acquisition Cost (CAC)
- **Average**: $15-25 per new customer
- **Range**: $10-40 depending on location and cuisine
- **Note**: Highly dependent on location, cuisine type, and competition

### Conversion Rates
- **Website to reservation**: 2-5%
- **Email open rate**: 20-25%
- **Email click-through**: 3-5%
- **Social media engagement**: 1-3%

### Retention Metrics
- **Repeat customer rate**: 30-40% (varies by restaurant type)
- **Average visits per customer**: 2-4 per year
- **Loyalty program participation**: 15-25% of customers

## Seasonal Considerations

### Peak Seasons
- **Holidays**: Thanksgiving, Christmas, New Year's Eve
- **Summer**: Outdoor dining, vacationers
- **Valentine's Day**: Couples dining
- **Mother's Day**: Family dining

### Slow Seasons
- **January-February**: Post-holiday slump
- **Late fall**: Weather-dependent for outdoor seating
- **Mid-week**: Typically slower than weekends

## Quick Wins

1. **Claim Google Business Profile**: Essential for local discovery
2. **Post 3-5 food photos this week**: Visual content performs best
3. **Set up email capture**: Simple form on website or in-store
4. **Ask 3 customers for reviews**: Social proof is critical
5. **Partner with 1 complementary business**: Cross-promotion
